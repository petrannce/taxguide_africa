Project Overview

The Engagement

TaxGuide Africa LLP was engaged by a multinational manufacturing group with intercompany transactions spanning Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania to prepare comprehensive transfer pricing documentation in compliance with the OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines and the domestic transfer pricing rules of all three East African jurisdictions.

The group had historically not maintained formal transfer pricing documentation, creating significant exposure following the increasing aggressiveness of revenue authorities — particularly the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) — in challenging intercompany pricing arrangements. The engagement was triggered when KRA issued a formal notice of intention to audit the group's transfer pricing arrangements.

The Challenge

The group had over 15 intercompany transaction categories across three jurisdictions with a combined value exceeding KSh 2.4 billion annually, no existing transfer pricing documentation, and an active KRA audit notice — all within a 90-day documentation deadline. The geographic complexity of three different domestic transfer pricing regimes required simultaneous expertise in Kenyan, Ugandan, and Tanzanian tax law.

Our Approach

TaxGuide Africa LLP deployed a multidisciplinary team led by Grace Wambui (Head of Tax Advisory) alongside transfer pricing specialists and legal advisors. Our approach proceeded in four concurrent workstreams:

  • Detailed transaction mapping across all 15 intercompany categories, including goods, services, financing, and IP licensing arrangements
  • Functional analysis of each entity within the group structure to determine appropriate economic characterisations
  • Comprehensive benchmarking studies using recognised commercial databases (Bureau van Dijk Orbis, TP Catalyst) to identify comparable uncontrolled transactions
  • Master File and Local File preparation in accordance with OECD BEPS Action 13, adapted for East African domestic requirements

Transfer Pricing Documentation Delivered

  • Group Master File covering global organisational structure, value chain analysis, and group-wide TP policies
  • Three jurisdiction-specific Local Files (Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania) with benchmarking analyses for all intercompany transactions
  • Country-by-Country Report (CbCR) filed with KRA in accordance with Finance Act requirements
  • Comprehensive written responses to KRA's audit queries with technical legal and economic arguments
  • TP Policy Manual for ongoing compliance and future-proofing of intercompany arrangements

KRA Audit Defence

Concurrent with the documentation exercise, our team managed the KRA audit through structured engagement — including a technical meeting with KRA Transfer Pricing Unit, preparation of detailed written responses to 23 audit queries, and presentation of benchmarking evidence. The audit concluded with no transfer pricing adjustments — a complete success for the client.
